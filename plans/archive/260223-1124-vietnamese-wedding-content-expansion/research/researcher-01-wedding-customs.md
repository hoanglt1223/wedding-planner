## 1. Ceremony Phases (5 Main Steps)

| Phase | Vietnamese | Participants | Notes |
|-------|-----------|--------------|-------|
| First visit | Lễ Dạm Ngõ | Immediate family only | Groom's family brings trầu cau, wine, sweets; informal |
| Engagement | Lễ Ăn Hỏi | Extended family + guests | Mâm quả procession; official betrothal |
| Pre-wedding | Lễ Xin Dâu | Parents only | Groom's mother visits to "ask" for bride |
| Wedding | Lễ Cưới (Vu Quy/Thành Hôn) | All guests | Tea ceremony, candle lighting, ring exchange |
| Post-wedding return | Lễ Lại Mặt | Couple + small gifts | Bride returns to parents' home (Day 2–3) |

**Lễ Rước Dâu** = procession from bride's home to groom's — occurs within Lễ Cưới day.

## 2. Regional Differences (Critical Data)

### Mâm Quả — Number of Trays

| Region | Quantity Rule | Common Counts | Logic |
|--------|--------------|---------------|-------|
| **Bắc (North)** | **Odd** numbers | 5, 7, 9, 11 | Odd = vitality/fortune |
| **Trung (Central)** | **Even** numbers (mixed) | 4, 6, 8 | Less emphasis on count |
| **Nam (South)** | **Even** numbers | 6, 8, 10 | Even = abundance/completeness |

> North rule nuance: "Lẻ ngoài, chẵn trong" — odd number of trays, even number of items inside each tray (e.g., 100 bánh, 8 bottles wine).

### Ceremony Protocol Differences

**North (Bắc):**
- Most formal; strict ritual order
- Lễ Dạm Ngõ, Lễ Ăn Hỏi, Lễ Xin Dâu, Lễ Cưới are kept as separate events
- Groom's family enters in seniority order
- Roasted suckling pig (heo quay) is standard

**Central (Trung):**
- Influenced by Nguyễn Dynasty royal customs (especially Huế)
- Unique ritual: "Giao Bôi Hợp Cẩn" — newlyweds chew betel leaves together, symbolizing harmony for 12 months/12 years
- No separate gift-giving tradition (unlike North)
- Calmer, more solemn tone; royal aesthetic

**South (Nam):**
- Most relaxed/flexible
- Lễ Ăn Hỏi + Lễ Cưới may be combined on same day if families live far apart
- Less rigid formality in ceremony order
- Even tray numbers reflect Southern preference for abundance symbolism

### Traditional Items per Region

| Item | North | Central | South |
|------|-------|---------|-------|
| Trầu cau | Required (always tray #1) | Required | Required (105 nuts, 210 leaves) |
| Rượu (wine) | Required | Required | Required |
| Bánh phu thê (husband-wife cakes) | Required | Required | Required |
| Bánh cốm (green sticky rice cake) | Required (Hanoi specialty) | Less common | Rare |
| Hạt sen (lotus seeds) | Common | Less common | Less common |
| Heo quay (roasted pig) | Common | Common | Common |
| Mứt sen (candied lotus) | Common in North | Rare | Rare |
| Dragon-phoenix candles | Required | Required (prominent) | Required |
| Tea set (bộ ấm trà) | Required | Required | Required |

### Dress Code Variations

| Region | Bride | Groom | Parents |
|--------|-------|-------|---------|
| North | Red áo dài; áo mớ ba mớ bảy (historical) | Blue/navy áo dài | Women: áo dài; men: áo dài + khăn xếp |
| Central (Huế) | Nhật Bình (royal áo dài, phoenix embroidered) | Áo dài with royal embroidery | Formal áo dài |
| South | Red or white áo dài (more flexible) | Suit or áo dài | Áo dài (flexible color) |

General: Red = luck; white increasingly accepted; guests avoid red, white, black.

## 3. Family Roles

| Role | Vietnamese | Function |
|------|-----------|----------|
| Matchmaker | Ông Mai / Bà Mai | Facilitates intro; present at Lễ Dạm Ngõ; receives tea first from groom |
| Groom's rep | Đại diện nhà trai | Delivers opening speech, presents gifts formally |
| Bride's rep | Đại diện nhà gái | Accepts gifts, delivers acceptance speech, opens trays |
| MC (Người dẫn chương trình) | MC / Người điều phối | Announces phases, introduces families, directs rituals |
| Parents | Cha mẹ cô dâu / chú rể | Sit at head positions; receive tea first (groom's parents, then bride's) |
| Gift bearers | Phù rể / Phù dâu | Carry mâm quả in procession; must be even number of bearers; unmarried preferred |

## 4. Traditional Items — Full Inventory

### Lễ Dạm Ngõ (minimal)
- Trầu cau (small bunch)
- Rượu (wine bottles)
- Bánh kẹo (sweets)

### Lễ Ăn Hỏi (full mâm quả)
- **Tray 1:** Trầu cau (betel + areca) — always present, symbolic of marriage origin legend
- **Tray 2:** Rượu + thuốc lá (wine + tobacco)
- **Tray 3:** Bánh phu thê / bánh su sê (husband-wife cakes)
- **Tray 4:** Hoa quả (seasonal fruit)
- **Tray 5:** Bộ ấm trà (tea set: teapot + 2 cups + tea leaves)
- **Tray 6+:** Hạt sen / mứt (lotus seeds / candied items), bánh cốm (North), heo quay
- **Dragon-phoenix candles** (nến đôi rồng phượng) — lit on ancestor altar
- **Jewelry** (vàng): rings, necklaces, bracelets — presented during ceremony
- **Lễ đen** (cash gift in red envelope) — from groom's family to bride's

### Lễ Cưới additions
- Bigger dragon-phoenix candles
- Full tea service for ceremony
- Wedding rings
- Champagne / wine for toast

## 5. Etiquette Rules

### Speech Order (Lễ Ăn Hỏi)
1. Đại diện nhà trai announces arrival and purpose
2. Mâm quả presented and accepted
3. Đại diện nhà gái accepts, opens trays, gives response speech
4. Items placed on ancestor altar; couple lights incense
5. Tea ceremony begins

### Tea Ceremony Procedure (Lễ Cưới)
1. Groom pours tea; bride holds tray
2. Offer to: groom's grandparents → groom's parents → bride's grandparents → bride's parents → matchmaker → other elders
3. Each elder drinks, then presents gift (jewelry or red envelope) + words of blessing
4. Couple lights dragon-phoenix candles
5. MC announces completion; family photos

### Seating
- Elders from groom's family sit on left (facing altar)
- Elders from bride's family sit on right
- Parents at front; grandparents center-front if present
- Altar must face main entrance (feng shui)

### Gift-Giving Protocol
- Red envelopes (bao lì xì / phong bì đỏ) for cash gifts
- Jewelry given publicly during tea ceremony (face-giving moment)
- Amount follows local custom; odd amounts (e.g., 500K, 1M, 3M VND) sometimes preferred in North; even in South
- Gifts from groom's family to bride's family = dowry discussion separate from mâm quả

### Procession (Lễ Rước Dâu)
- Groom arrives at bride's home at auspicious time (chosen by fortune teller / lịch vạn niên)
- Even number of people in procession
- Groom enters first; presents flowers to bride
- Couple bows to bride's family altar together
- Procession walks to groom's home or wedding venue
